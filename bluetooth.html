What is Bluetooth?

Bluetooth is a wireless personal area network technology that allows devices to communicate with each other over short distances, typically within a range of 30 feet (10 meters).

Bluetooth uses radio waves to transmit data between devices, and operates on the 2.4 GHz frequency band.

Bluetooth is used in a wide range of devices, including headphones, speakers, smartphones, and laptops!

A Brief History of Bluetooth

Bluetooth was invented in 1994 by a team of engineers at Intel, led by Jaap Haartsen and Sven Mattison.

How Bluetooth Works

  • Devices with Bluetooth capabilities have a small radio transmitter and receiver.
  • When a device is turned on, it begins to broadcast a unique identifier, called a MAC address.
  • Other devices in range can detect the broadcast and initiate a connection.
  • Data is transmitted between devices using a technique called frequency hopping spread spectrum.

Advantages of Bluetooth

  • Wireless connectivity eliminates the need for cables.
  • Bluetooth devices are often low-power and low-cost.
  • The technology is widely supported by many devices and operating systems.
